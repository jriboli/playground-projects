Clinical Study Assist Cookie Policy

1. WHAT IS A COOKIE?

A cookie is a text file that is stored on the hard drive of your device (computer, tablet, mobile) through your browser software when you visit an online service Cookies do not store any sensitive or directly identifying personal data but simply provide information about your navigation so that your device can be recognized later. Cookies do not cause any damage to your device, but make it easier, for example, to retrieve your preferences, to pre-fill certain fields and to adapt the content of our services. You alone choose whether you wish to have cookies saved on your device and you can easily control the saving of cookies. For specific information on the management and control of cookies, please refer to the section "Your choices regarding cookies".

2. WHICH COOKIES DO WE USE?

Analytics cookies The analytics cookies enable us to analyze the use of our website or mobile site and to improve our user journey by establishing statistics for volumes of frequentation and use (headings and content visited, journeys, etc.). These cookies are stored for a period of 24 months.

3. YOUR CHOICES REGARDING COOKIES

Setting up your browser or mobile device At any time, you may express or modify your wishes regarding cookies, using the following procedures: You can set your browser at any time so that cookies are stored on your terminal (opt-in) or, on the contrary, so as to reject them (opt-out). Some browsers allow you to set rules to manage cookies on a site-by-site basis. Each browser is differently designed to manage cookies. It is described in the help menu of your browser, which will allow you to know how to modify your wishes: Internet Explorer Safari Chrome Firefox In addition, the procedure for turning off ad targeting on your mobile devices is as follows: for iOS, go to « Settings », scroll down until you find « Privacy » then turn on « Limit Ad Tracking »; for Android, go to « Settings » > « Privacy » > « Advanced » > « Ads » and then toggle out of Ads Personalization.
